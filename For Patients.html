Consultation

  1. How do I fix an appointment at Apex eye Clinic and what information should I provide?

    You can fix an appointment by calling on 020-65006768 or 9011494300. Provide the name of the patient, age and primary problem.

  2. What do I need to carry while coming for my appointment?

    You should carry your previous prescription glasses if any, or previous old spectacles or contact lenses. Any blood reports that you might have done recently and if any medicine prescription or medicine if you taking for same problem or any other health problem. To get the data about it because it may have relation with your persisting problem, and it will guide us to treat you in a better way and avoid your unnecessary frequent visits.

  3. If I am contact lens user, what measures should I take before visiting the clinic?

    You should be off the contact lenses for at least 24hrs before coming for consultation if you made a prior appointment and also carry your contact lenses along with you kit at the time of the appointment, to know the status of your contact lenses.

    If in emergency consultation, you should carry your old glasses and contact lens kit.

  4. How much time do I need for consultation?

    You need at least 15 mins to maximum 45-60 mins if you need a dilated retinal examination.

For Children below 14 years

  1. At what age should children have their first eye examination?

    It is recommended that children should undergo their first eye examination by the age of 3 years. But if you notice that your child is facing any difficulty or any problem, visit at the earliest. In children, they should consult as early as possible to rule out the disease and treat the eye problem at the earliest stage to avoid further complications.

  2. What type of eye examinations are done for children?

    For children, vision is checked by using a special method. Also, dilated retinal examination and refraction is mandatory in children below 14 years of age by putting dilating eye drops or ointments as per the requirement and according to the age of the kid.

  3. My child sits very close to the T.V. or holds book very close to face while reading. Is it harmful?

    No, it is not harmful, only it will induce eye strain. But it indicates that your child is might be facing vision problem so do consult ophthalmologist.

  4. What is a lazy eye?

    Medical term for lazy eye is Amblyopia. It is an eye condition noted by reduced vision, not correctable by glasses or contact lenses and it is not due any eye disease. Treatment is possible at early stages i.e., developmental stages of life which is below 15years of age as early as possible.

Contact lenses or LASIK

  1. When can a person use contact lenses or go for a LASIK surgery?

    After the age of 18 years if your spectacle number is constant over the last one year, you can think about LASIK surgery provided, your retina and cornea are normal.

    For contact lenses, it is advisable to use after the age of 15-16 years as a person is mature enough to take care of the lenses and can handle them properly.

  2. Which type of lenses are good?

    Mostly all types of contact lenses are good. They are made for different conditions. Doctor can decide which type of contact lenses will be suitable for your eyes after a proper eye examination that will yield best results.

  3. Comparatively, which is better- contact lenses or LASIK?

    Both LASIK and contact lenses carry their own advantages and disadvantages or limitations. Ophthalmologist can decide which option is better for your eyes after an examination.
